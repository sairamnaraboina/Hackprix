Explore The Unexplored in Summer

Araku Valley

Araku Valley is a hill station and valley region in the southeastern Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. It's surrounded by the thick forests of the Eastern Ghats mountain range. The Tribal Museum is dedicated to the area's numerous indigenous tribes, known for their traditional Dhimsa dance, and showcases traditional handicrafts. A miniature train runs through Padmapuram Gardens, with its sculptures and tree-top huts.

Darjeeling

Darjeeling, a crown jewel of India, sits amidst the Himalayas, famed for its endless rows of verdant tea estates. This charming town offers breathtaking views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can experience the unique culture by riding the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, a delightful toy train, and savor a steaming cup of world-renowned Darjeeling tea.

Coorg

Coorg, officially known as Kodagu, is the most affluent hill station in Karnataka. It is well known for its breathtakingly exotic scenery and lush greenery. Forest covered hills, spice and coffee plantations only add to the landscape.

Munnar

Munnar, is a breathtaking hill station nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala. Carpeted with sprawling tea plantations that stretch across emerald slopes, Munnar offers a visual feast for nature lovers. Beyond the tea estates, cascading waterfalls like Echo Point and scenic lakes like Kundala Lake add to the region's charm. Adventure seekers can trek through Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, or embark on a thrilling mountain biking expedition. Munnar's cool climate and serene atmosphere make it a perfect escape for relaxation and rejuvenation, allowing you to truly unwind amidst the captivating beauty of the South Indian mountains.
