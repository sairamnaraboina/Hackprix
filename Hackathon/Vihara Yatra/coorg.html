Coorg

Description

Nestled amidst the lush Western Ghats, Coorg, also known as the "Scotland of India," is a captivating hill station. This coffee paradise boasts rolling green hills draped in coffee plantations, offering a visual treat and an invigorating aroma. Adventure enthusiasts can trek through verdant forests, while history buffs can explore Madikeri Fort, a reminder of the region's rich past. Waterfalls like Abbey Falls cascade down the hills, adding a touch of serenity. Coorg's charm extends beyond its beauty; the Kodavas, a local warrior clan known for their hospitality, warmly welcome visitors. Indulge in a cup of locally brewed coffee and savor the unique cuisine to complete your Coorg experience.

A Taste of coorg: Local Cuisine Coorg cuisine, also known as Kodava cuisine, is a vibrant tapestry woven with flavors reflecting the region's geography, history, and culture. Nestled in the Western Ghats, Coorg draws inspiration from its surroundings. Rice, the plentiful crop, forms the base for many dishes, while spices like cardamom, pepper, and indigenous herbs add a unique flair.

1. Pandi Curry:
The undisputed king of Coorg cuisine, this slow-cooked pork curry is a flavorful and aromatic dish.
It's a must-try for any non-vegetarian visitor.
2. Kadambuttu:
Steamed rice dumplings are a comforting and popular choice, often enjoyed with pandi curry or other flavorful curries.
3. Kootu Curry:
A vibrant mixed vegetable curry simmered in coconut milk, offering a flavorful and healthy option.
4. Ellu Pajji:
A unique and flavorful chutney made with sesame seeds, perfect for dipping or adding a nutty taste to your meal.

Local Traditions of coorg

  1. Warrior Spirit: The Kodavas, Coorg's indigenous people, are known for their martial spirit. Their festivals and ceremonies often reflect this heritage, with displays of swordsmanship (known as Kuttiyatthur) and a strong sense of community.
  2. Unique customs: Unlike most of India, inheritance in Coorg traditionally passes from mother to daughter, highlighting the importance given to women in Kodava society. Widows are allowed to remarry, another social custom that sets Coorg apart.
  3. Nature worship: The Kodavas revere nature and have a deep respect for the environment. They worship rivers, forests, and ancestral spirits, reflecting their harmonious relationship with the land.

Must Visit Places

  • Abbey Falls:
    This cascading waterfall surrounded by lush greenery is a popular spot for picnicking and enjoying the beauty of nature.
  • Bear Shola Falls:
    Raja's Seat: Offering panoramic views of the Coorg landscape, this spot was a favorite among the Kodava kings.
  • Tadiandamol Peak:
    The highest peak in Coorg, Tadiandamol is a paradise for trekkers. The trek offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Nagarhole National Park:
    A haven for wildlife enthusiasts, Nagarhole National Park is home to tigers, elephants, leopards, and a variety of birds.
