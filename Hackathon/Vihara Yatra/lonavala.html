LONAVALA

Description

Lonavala, the "Jewel of the Sahyadris," isn't a beach destination, but a nature lover's paradise nestled in the Western Ghats. Towering mountains clad in emerald greenery and cascading waterfalls create a breathtaking backdrop. The cool mountain air offers a refreshing escape from the heat, perfect for relaxation. Explore the ancient Buddhist caves carved into cliffs like Karla and Bhaja, or challenge yourself on scenic treks. Foodies can indulge in the local chikki candy and savor a cup of chai with a view. While Lonavala itself lacks a beach, its location makes it a great base to explore nearby coastal gems. Kashid Beach offers pristine waters and water sports, while Alibaug Beach boasts a relaxed vibe and historical forts. Lonavala promises a refreshing escape amidst mountains, historical wonders, and the potential for a seaside adventure.

A Taste of Lonavala: Local Cuisine Lonavala tantalizes taste buds with its local specialties!

1. Chikki
Lonavala's most famous sweet treat, chikki is a melt-in-your-mouth candy made with nuts and jaggery.
These delicious squares come in various nut and flavor combinations, making them perfect for gifting or enjoying yourself.
2. Misal Pav
This spicy lentil curry with a unique blend of sprouts and vegetables is a popular Maharashtrian dish.
Traditionally served with pav (bread), misal pav is a delightful combination of savory and spicy flavors.
3. Vada Pav
A ubiquitous street food snack across Maharashtra, vada pav is a deep-fried potato patty sandwiched in a bread roll.
The flavorful potato filling and the option to add chutneys make it a satisfying and budget-friendly snack.
4. Strawberry Products
Due to its proximity to Mahabaleshwar, known for its strawberries, Lonavala offers a variety of fresh and processed strawberry products.
Enjoy fresh strawberries with cream, sip on a cool glass of strawberry milkshake, or indulge in delectable strawberry chocolates.

Local Traditions of Lonavala

  1. Chikki Making: Traditional sweet made of jaggery and nuts.
  2. Festivals: Vibrant celebrations of Diwali, Ganesh Chaturthi, and Holi.
  3. Local Cuisine: Diverse dishes reflecting Maharashtrian, Gujarati, and Parsi influences.
  4. Arts and Crafts: Skilled artisans preserving pottery, bamboo craft, and Warli painting.
  5. Monsoon Rituals: Special ceremonies to honor rain gods during the rainy season.
  6. Outdoor Activities: Trekking, camping, and waterfall rappelling in the lush landscape.
  7. Hospitality and Community Spirit: Warm welcome and strong community bonds among residents.

Travel Options to Lonavala

1. By Air

GPune International Airport
  • Location: 56 km from Lonavala.
  • Flights: Regular domestic and international flights.
  • Major Airlines: Air India, Indigo, SpiceJet, GoAir, Vistara, and international carriers.
  • Connectivity: Direct flights from major Indian cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai) and international destinations (Middle East, Europe).
Transport from Airports:
  • Taxis: Prepaid and metered taxis available.
  • Buses: Local and private buses connect the airport to major destinations.
  • Car Rentals: Available at the airport.

2. By Rail

Key Routes:
  • The nearest railway station is Lonavala Railway Station, located about 8 kilometers away in the neighboring town of Wadgaon.
  • South Western Railway: Links Goa with Bangalore and other cities in Karnataka.
Trains:
  • Deccan Express (11007), Sinhagad Express (11009) : Popular trains from Mumbai.
  • Sahyadri Express (11024), Koyna Express (11030) : Connects pune with Lonavala.
  • Konark Express (11020), Mumbai Express (22731): Connects hyderabad and Lonavala.
Transport from Stations:
  • Taxis and Auto Rickshaws: Readily available.
  • Buses: Frequent bus services to various parts of Lonavala.

3. By Road

National Highways:
  • National Highway 48 (NH48):This is the Mumbai-Pune Expressway, a six-lane highway considered the most convenient route to reach Lonavala by car. It is well-maintained and offers a faster travel time compared to other routes.
Buses:
  • Interstate Buses: The MSRTC (Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation) buses ply regularly between Lonavala and Lonavala Railway Station. The journey takes about 20-30 minutes depending on traffic. You can find the bus stop near the main market area in Lonavala.
Self-Drive:
  • Car Rentals: Available in major cities and tourist spots.
  • Motorbikes and Scooters: Popular for local travel, easily rented in Goa.

Must Visit Places

  • Karla Caves:
    Explore the ancient Karla Caves, a complex of Buddhist cave monuments dating back to the 2nd century BC. Carved into a hillside, these caves feature intricate sculptures and stunning architecture, offering a glimpse into Buddhist history.
  • Bhaja Caves:
    Another must-see for history buffs are the Bhaja Caves, located about a kilometer away from Karla Caves. These caves, also dating back to the 2nd century BC, showcase beautiful carvings and a serene atmosphere.
  • Tiger's Leap:
    For a breathtaking view, head to Tiger's Leap, a popular viewpoint overlooking the valley below. Legend has it that a tiger leaped across the valley here, giving rise to the name. Enjoy stunning panoramic views, especially during monsoons when the waterfalls are cascading.
  • Lonavala Lake:
    Lonavala Lake is a picturesque spot perfect for a leisurely boat ride or a stroll along the promenade. Enjoy the serene ambiance and capture beautiful photographs with the lake and mountains as your backdrop.
  • VWax Museum:
    If you're looking for some indoor entertainment, visit the Sunil's Celebrity Wax Museum. Take pictures with life-size wax figures of famous personalities from Bollywood, Hollywood, and sports.
