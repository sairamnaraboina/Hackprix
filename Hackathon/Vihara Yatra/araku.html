Arakau Valley

Description

Feel the essence of the Araku Valley, the nature potrying its beauty.Araku Valley is a hill station and valley region in the southeastern Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. It's surrounded by the thick forests of the Eastern Ghats mountain range.

"Araku Valley: Experience the Rich Tapestry of Tribal Heritage and Nature's Bounty, One Flavorful Bite at a Time"

1. Seafood Specialties
Fish Curry: Prepared with local spices and herbs, often featuring fresh river fish.
Dry Fish Recipes: Utilized in various traditional dishes, where fish is dried and then cooked with spices.
2. Meat and Poultry
Bamboo-Chicken(Bongulo Chicken): Marinated chicken cooked inside bamboo tubes over an open fire, giving it a unique flavor.
Mutton Curry: Made with tender goat meat and local spices, slow-cooked to perfection.
Royyala Vepudu: Spicy prawn fry, marinated and shallow-fried, is sometimes featured in areas with access to coastal seafood.
3. Vegetarian Delights
Pachadi: A variety of chutneys made from local vegetables, herbs, and sometimes fruits, like tomato pachadi or gongura pachadi.
Samai Rice(Little Millet): Served with various local vegetable curries, it's a staple grain in the region.
Jonna Rotte (Sorghum Roti): Flatbreads made from sorghum flour, typically paired with vegetable dishes.
4. Snacks and Street Food
Chegodilu: Crunchy, deep-fried rings made from rice flour and flavored with sesame seeds and spices. A popular tea-time snack.
Sajja Boorelu: Deep-fried sweet dumplings made from pearl millet (sajja) and jaggery. A traditional snack enjoyed during festivals.
5. Beverages
Araku Coffee: Renowned for its rich flavor and aroma, made from organic coffee beans grown locally in the valley's plantations.
Traditional Herbal Drinks: Made from locally foraged herbs and millets, offering unique flavors and health benefits.
6. Sweets and Desserts
Ariselu: Sweet made from rice flour and jaggery, deep-fried to a golden brown.
Pootarekulu: Also known as "paper sweet," made from thin layers of rice starch, ghee, and sugar or jaggery.
Bobbatlu: Sweet stuffed flatbreads made with chana dal, jaggery, and cardamom, typically prepared during festivals and special occasions.

Local Traditions of Araku Valley

  • Tribal Art and Handicrafts: Exquisite pottery, bamboo craft, and traditional jewelry reflect cultural heritage.
  • Dance and Music: Tribal dance forms and traditional instruments express cultural identity.
  • Festivals and Celebrations: Colorful rituals and community gatherings mark significant events.
  • Traditional Attire: Distinctive clothing with vibrant colors and intricate patterns.
  • Cuisine and Food Culture: Locally grown produce and traditional cooking methods.
  • Spiritual Beliefs: Deep-rooted connections with nature, ancestors, and sacred sites.
  • Community Bonding: Emphasis on mutual cooperation and shared responsibility.

Travel Options to Araku Valley

1. By Air

Visakhapatnam Airport (VTZ)
  • Location: The nearest airport to Araku Valley is Visakhapatnam Airport, which is well-connected to major cities across India. After reaching Visakhapatnam, travelers can hire a taxi or board a bus to Araku Valley.
  • Flights: Regular domestic and international flights.
  • Major Airlines: Air India, Indigo, SpiceJet, GoAir, Vistara, and international carriers.
  • Connectivity: Direct flights from major Indian cities (Vishakapatnam)

2. By Rail

Major Railway Stations:
  • Visakhapatnam to Araku Valley Train: The Visakhapatnam-Kirandul Passenger train, also known as the Vistadome train, offers a scenic rail journey from Visakhapatnam to Araku Valley.
Key Routes:
  • Visakhapatnam (VSKP): Well-connected to major cities across India, including Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, and Delhi.
  • Araku (ARK): The train journey from Visakhapatnam to Araku offers breathtaking views of the Eastern Ghats, including lush green valleys, cascading waterfalls, and picturesque tunnels.
Trains:
  • Vishakapatnam-Secunderabad Garib Rath Express: Key Stops: Vizianagaram, Samalkot, Rajahmundry, Vijayawada, Secunderabad
  • Simhadri Express: Key Stops: Vizianagaram, Srikakulam Road, Palasa, Brahmapur, Bhubaneswar, Balasore, Kharagpur, Howrah
  • Vishakapatnam-Tirupati Weekly Express: Key Stops: Vizianagaram, Srikakulam Road, Palasa, Brahmapur, Bhubaneswar, Balasore, Kharagpur, Vijayawada, Guntur, Renigunta, Tirupati
Transport from Stations:
  • Taxis and Auto Rickshaws: Readily available.
  • Buses: Frequent bus services to various parts of Vishakapatnam.
  • Car Rentals: Available near major stations.

3. By Road

National Highways:
  • National Highway 516 (NH516): National Highway 516 (NH516) connects Visakhapatnam to Araku Valley, passing through Anantagiri, Araku, Padmapuram Gardens, Borra Caves, and terminating near Sileru close to the Chhattisgarh border.
Buses:
  • APSRTC Buses: The Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation (APSRTC) operates regular bus services from Visakhapatnam to Araku Valley.
Self-Drive:
  • Car Rentals: Available in major cities and tourist spots.
  • Motorbikes and Scooters: Popular for local travel, easily rented in Araku Valley.

Must-Visit Places in Araku Valley

  • Borra Caves: Explore massive limestone formations and unique geological features.
  • Tribal Museum: Discover the rich culture and heritage of the indigenous tribes of the Eastern Ghats.
  • Padmapuram Gardens: Relax in beautiful gardens with a toy train ride and scenic views.
  • Ananthagiri Hills: Trek through lush forests and coffee plantations, enjoying serene landscapes.
  • Galikonda Viewpoint: Take in panoramic vistas of Araku Valley from a high vantage point.
  • Sangda Waterfall: Trek to a picturesque waterfall amidst tranquil surroundings.
  • Coffee Plantations: Learn about coffee cultivation and enjoy freshly brewed coffee.
  • Chaparai Water Cascade: Relax by cascading waters in a serene natural setting.
  • Dumbriguda Waterfalls: Marvel at a majestic waterfall cascading from great heights.
  • Matsyagundam: Visit a serene pond believed to be inhabited by sacred fish.
  • Gangaraju Madugula: Witness a natural rock formation resembling a frog amidst scenic beauty.
  • Araku Tribal Dance Performances: Experience vibrant tribal culture through traditional dance performances.
