This "Princess of Hill Stations" offers a cool escape from the Indian heat, with temperatures averaging around 19Â°C. Explore shimmering Kodaikanal Lake by boat, trek through lush forests, or visit Bear Shola Falls for a refreshing cascade. Whether you seek relaxation or adventure, Kodaikanal's scenic beauty and pleasant climate provide an unforgettable experience.

A Taste of Kodaikanal: Local Cuisine Kodaikanal's cuisine is a delightful tapestry woven from South Indian staples and Tibetan influences.

1. Idli & Dosa:
South Indian staples, these fluffy rice and lentil cakes are a delicious and healthy breakfast option.
They are typically served with sambar (a lentil stew) and chutneys (coconut, tomato, or mint).
2. Momos:
A delicious Tibetan import, momos are steamed dumplings filled with savory vegetables or meat.
They are enjoyed with a spicy chutney or dipping sauce.
3. Murtabak:
A flaky flatbread stuffed with vegetables, meat, or eggs, murtabak is a popular street food.
It's often served with a lentil soup or curry.
4. Chocolate:
Kodaikanal is famous for its homemade chocolates.
Indulge in a variety of flavors, from dark and milk chocolate to fruit and nut-filled options.

Local Traditions of kodaikonal

  1. Martial Arts: Kalaripayattu, a traditional martial art form from Kerala, is practiced in some parts of Kodaikanal. You might see training sessions or even cultural performances showcasing this energetic art form.
  2. Festivals:Kodaikanal vibrates with colors and festivities throughout the year. Major festivals like Pongal (harvest festival), Summer Festival (floral exhibition), and Christmas are celebrated with joy. You might witness vibrant processions, traditional performances, and a festive atmosphere.
  3. Shola Grass handicrafts: The shola forests surrounding Kodaikanal provide materials for creating intricate handicrafts. Look for souvenirs made from shola grass, a type of fast-growing reed, showcasing the local craft traditions..
  4. Boat Races: During the summer months, boat races are a popular local tradition on Kodaikanal Lake. Teams compete in colorful boats, creating an exciting atmosphere.

Travel Options to kodaikanal

1. By Air

GPune International Airport
  • Location: 120 km from kodaikanal.The nearest airport is Madurai Airport (IXM), located about 120 kilometers away. Regular flights connect Madurai to major cities in India, making it a convenient option for reaching Kodaikanal. The travel time from Madurai Airport to Kodaikanal by taxi or bus is around 3-4 hours.
  • Flights: Regular domestic and international flights.
  • Major Airlines: Air India, Indigo, SpiceJet, GoAir, Vistara, and international carriers.
  • Connectivity: Direct flights from major Indian cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai) and international destinations (Middle East, Europe).
Transport from Airports:
  • Taxis: Prepaid and metered taxis available.
  • Buses: Local and private buses connect the airport to major destinations.
  • Car Rentals: Available at the airport.

2. By Rail

Key Routes:
  • Kodai Road Railway Station: This station serves as the nearest railhead for Kodaikanal, situated approximately 80-90 kilometers away. Many major cities in India have trains connecting to Kodai Road Railway Station. This makes it a feasible option to reach Kodaikanal by train and then continue your journey by car, bus, or taxi to the hill station itself.
  • Kodai Road Station: Once you reach Kodai Road Railway Station, you can take a taxi, bus, or pre-booked cab to reach your final destination in Kodaikanal. The journey by road typically takes about 2-3 hours depending on traffic conditions..
Trains:
  • Kodai Road Railway Station (KQN): From all other states
Transport from Stations:
  • Taxis and Auto Rickshaws: Readily available.
  • Buses: Frequent bus services to various parts of Lonavala.

  • NH45 (National Highway 45): origin city till Madurai. Then, from Madurai, you can take State Highway 156 (SH 156) which leads directly to Kodaikanal. This is a popular route for reaching Kodaikanal by road.

Must Visit Places

  • Kodaikanal Lake:
    The star attraction, this star-shaped lake is perfect for boating, offering stunning views and a serene ambiance.
  • Bear Shola Falls:
    Bear Shola Falls: Embark on a short trek through a beautiful shola forest (a type of evergreen forest) to reach these cascading waterfalls, perfect for a refreshing dip.
  • Silver Cascade Falls:
    Cascading down granite rocks amidst lush greenery, these falls are another scenic spot for nature enthusiasts.
  • Guna Caves:
    Explore these naturally formed caves with historical significance, said to have been inhabited by prehistoric people.
  • Pillar Rocks:
    Hike up to these towering rock formations for breathtaking views and a thrilling experience for nature lovers.
